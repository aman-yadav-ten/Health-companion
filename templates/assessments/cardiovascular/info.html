Cardiovascular Disease

What is Cardiovascular Disease?

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) refers to conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. It is commonly caused by a buildup of fatty deposits in arteries and significantly increases the risk of heart attack and stroke.

Major Types of CVD

  • Coronary Heart Disease: Reduced blood flow to the heart muscle.
  • Stroke & TIA: Disrupted blood supply to the brain.
  • Peripheral Arterial Disease: Blocked arteries in the legs.
  • Aortic Disease: Weakening or enlargement of the aorta.

Medical Risk Factors

  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Diabetes
  • Heart disease

Lifestyle Risk Factors

  • Smoking
  • Physical inactivity
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Excessive alcohol intake

Non-Modifiable Risk Factors

  • Age
  • Gender
  • Family history
  • Ethnic background
