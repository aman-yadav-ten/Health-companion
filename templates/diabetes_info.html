Diabetes Information

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic medical condition characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose. Over time, unmanaged diabetes can lead to serious complications affecting the heart, kidneys, eyes, and nervous system.

Why Diabetes is Dangerous

Persistent high blood sugar damages blood vessels and nerves, significantly increasing the risk of cardiovascular disease, vision loss, kidney failure, and limb complications.

Type 1 Diabetes

An autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little or no insulin. It commonly develops in children and young adults.

Type 2 Diabetes

The most common form of diabetes. The body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough of it. Strongly linked to lifestyle factors.

Prediabetes

A warning stage where blood glucose levels are higher than normal but not yet diabetic. Early intervention can prevent progression.
